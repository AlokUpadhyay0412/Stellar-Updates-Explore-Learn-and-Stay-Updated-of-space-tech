What is Astronomy?

Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole. It covers a wide range of topics, including stars, planets, galaxies, and cosmic phenomena.

Stars

Stars are giant balls of gas that produce light and heat. The closest star to Earth is the Sun, which is essential for life on our planet.

Nebula

A nebula is a vast cloud of gas and dust in space, often the birthplace of stars. They are beautiful and mysterious parts of the universe.

Planets

Planets are large celestial bodies that orbit stars. Our solar system has eight planets, with Earth being the only one known to support life.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. It has a diverse environment, including oceans, mountains, and forests.

Galaxies

Galaxies are massive systems of stars, planets, and dust held together by gravity. Our galaxy is the Milky Way, which contains billions of stars.

Andromeda Galaxy

The Andromeda Galaxy is the closest large galaxy to the Milky Way and is on a collision course with us, but this event is billions of years away.
